POSITIONS

What are the different positions?

Baseball has nine different fielding (defense) positions. Pitcher (1), catcher (2), first base (3), second base (4), shortstop (5), third base (6), center field (7), right field (8), and left field (9). First base, second base, shortstop, and third base are part of the infield positions. Center field, left field, and right field are part of the outfield positions. All positions require a specific set of skills. The catcher’s job is to catch the balls that the pitcher throws. The catcher has the responsibility of calling pitches by giving signs to the pitcher which pitch to throw. The catcher is also responsible for things like blocking, throwing out runners, and covering the home plate. Another important position is the “designated hitter” also known as the DH position. This player does not have a position during defense and only bats.
