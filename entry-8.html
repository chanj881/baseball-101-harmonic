UMPIRES

What is the role of an umpire?

The umpires, also known as the umps, “are responsible for enforcing on-field rules and rendering decisions on judgement calls such as: whether a batter or baserunner is safe or out, and whether a pitched baseball is a strike or ball” (MLB Glossary). Each game has four umpires, one at home plate and one at each base. Umps are also in charge of determining if balls fall into fair or foul territories in the field. Additionally, umpires “have the jurisdiction to eject platers, coaches or managers who break certain rules or do not display proper on-field conduct” (MLB Glossary).
